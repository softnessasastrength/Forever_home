# Building Program

## Status

Conceptual owner requirements. This document is not permit-ready and must later be validated by an architect, structural engineer, mechanical engineer, electrical designer, utility provider, and local code officials.

## Overall Form

The baseline architectural form is a raised-ranch-inspired home with no more than three functional levels:

1. Finished basement / lower level
2. Main living floor
3. Upper private floor

The exact split-level geometry may evolve during schematic design. The intent is to preserve efficient vertical separation between flexible, public, and private spaces without creating a tall or overly complicated house.

## Size

- Approximate center target: 1,500 sq ft above grade
- The target is intentionally flexible
- The final area may be smaller or larger when justified by circulation, room usability, accessibility, structure, or systems
- The governing principle is intentional square footage rather than hitting an arbitrary number

Finished-basement area will be tracked separately from above-grade living area.

## Lower Level / Finished Basement

The lower level shall be finished from initial construction and planned to support future use as an in-law, long-term guest, or semi-independent living area.

Required or preferred spaces:

- Direct interior access from the garage
- Separate exterior entry where site conditions allow
- Walk-out configuration preferred when the lot supports it
- Full bathroom with toilet, sink, and shower
- Separate enclosed laundry room
- Flexible living / recreation area
- Bedroom-capable room or future sleeping room, subject to legal egress and local requirements
- Mechanical room
- Electrical and energy equipment area
- Dedicated low-voltage / technology closet or rack area
- General storage
- Pellet storage area with practical access from the garage or exterior

The basement should be able to function with meaningful independence while remaining connected to the rest of the home.

## Main Living Floor

The main floor is the emotional and social center of the house.

Core spaces:

- Front entry
- Living room
- Kitchen
- Dining area
- Three-season porch accessed from the dining area
- Pantry or high-capacity kitchen storage
- Powder room or conveniently located bathroom
- Covered front porch
- Rear deck, covered patio, or garden access
- Pellet stove located in the main living area

The living, dining, and kitchen spaces should feel connected without becoming one undifferentiated room. The design should preserve cozy proportions, visual warmth, and places for furniture, collections, books, lamps, and daily rituals.

Dimensional schematic specs for kitchen, dining, and three-season porch: [Kitchen_Dining_Porch_Specs.md](Kitchen_Dining_Porch_Specs.md).

### Vertical circulation

Half-story stair runs (**five to seven steps**, preferred **six risers**) connect lower ↔ main and main ↔ upper private levels. Baseline: carpeted treads, traditional turned-baluster railings. See [Stairs_Half_Story.md](Stairs_Half_Story.md).

## Upper Private Floor

Core spaces:

- Primary bedroom
- Renn's dedicated boutique / dressing room
- Walk-in closet directly connected to or integrated with the boutique
- Branden's dedicated home office
- Minimum of two additional legal bedrooms across the whole house program so the home contains at least three bedrooms total
- Guest bedroom
- Full bathroom or bathrooms as developed during planning

The office and boutique are equal-priority personal spaces and must not be treated as leftover storage rooms.

## Garage

- Attached garage preferred
- Direct protected connection to the lower level
- At least one EV-charging parking position
- EVSE planned on the left-side garage wall
- Wall blocking, conduit, circuit pathway, and cable-management clearance included from the beginning
- Space for a garage electrical subpanel
- Practical access to pellet storage
- Utility/service wall preferably located on or near the garage side of the house

## Accessibility and Long-Term Adaptability

The design should anticipate aging, injury, and changing household needs.

Planning considerations:

- Wider critical doorways where practical
- Clear circulation paths
- A lower-level bathroom with shower
- Potential future stair lift, platform lift, or compact elevator pathway
- Stacked or coordinated plumbing zones
- Avoiding unnecessary level changes beyond the raised-ranch organization
- Maintaining future options rather than committing to inaccessible dead ends

## Aesthetic Direction

The architectural character is a whimsical grandma home: warm, collected, comfortable, gently eccentric, and designed to feel as though it has been cared for over time.

The raised-ranch structure should not produce a dark, generic, closed-off interior. Natural light, built-ins, warm materials, useful nooks, layered lighting, and human-scaled rooms should soften the efficient structural form.
