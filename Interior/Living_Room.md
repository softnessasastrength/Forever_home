# Living Room Concept

**Aesthetic:** Warm, welcoming, nostalgic — whimsical grandma home  
**Role:** Emotional and social center of the main living floor  
**Status:** Concept direction (not construction-ready)  
**Concept render:** [Renderings/Living_Room_Concept.jpg](../Renderings/Living_Room_Concept.jpg)

## Intent

The living room should feel like arriving somewhere you are already loved: soft light, familiar shapes, room for two to sit close without the space feeling sparse or staged. It supports evening TV, quiet reading, conversation with the kitchen nearby, and that first warm view when you come through the front door.

## Focal wall: TV + faux fireplace

**Main wall composition (top to bottom):**

1. **Large flat-screen TV** — wall-mounted, centered on the primary wall; the room’s media focus
2. **Fake fireplace directly underneath** — electric or decorative insert with mantel; provides the visual and emotional “hearth” without requiring a real chimney on this wall

### Design notes

- Mantel should feel traditional and collectible: wood or painted millwork with enough depth for photos, candles, small frames, and seasonal objects
- Keep the TV / fireplace stack as one centered vertical composition so the wall reads as a hearth media wall, not two competing objects
- Prefer warm surround materials (wood tones, soft cream or sage paint, brick or tile surround if used) over cold black glass and gray stone
- Hide cables; remote and device storage can live in a low console, built-in niches beside the surround, or baskets under the mantel
- Coordinate with whole-house heating strategy (Building Program may still use a pellet stove elsewhere in the main living area — this living-room unit is for ambiance and secondary comfort, not the sole heat source unless later decided)

## Seating

| Piece | Size / type | Placement | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loveseat | Moderately sized (approx. 2-seat) | Facing or slightly angled toward the TV / fireplace wall | Shared seating for two; primary “together” spot |
| La-Z-Boy recliner | Full recliner | Immediately next to the loveseat | Individual comfort, napping, long TV sessions; stays conversationally connected to the loveseat |

### Layout principles

- Keep the loveseat and recliner as a **paired seating group**, not isolated islands — arm-to-arm or with a small shared side table between them
- Leave clear circulation from the front entry arch into the seating zone and toward kitchen / dining
- Soft textiles: throws, cushions, possibly a patterned or solid area rug under the seating group
- Side table(s) and lamp within reach of both seats for drinks, remote, and reading

## Entry: grand archway

**Above / framing the front door entryway into the living room:** a large, grand, inviting archway or decorative trim.

### Intent

- First impression from the porch and threshold should feel open-armed, not a plain drywall rectangle
- Arch or enriched casing should read traditional and welcoming — soft curve preferred over sharp modern portal
- Scale should feel **grand relative to a modest house**, without museum-scale formality
- Pair with warm paint, soft shadow lines, and possibly subtle decorative molding so the entry feels intentional and storied

Options to develop later: full radius arch, elliptical arch, or square opening with deep decorative head casing and corbels if a true arch conflicts with structure or head height.

## Lighting concept (layered)

Use three complementary systems so the room works morning through late night without harsh single-source light.

| Layer | Type | Character | Control |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daylight | **Skylights** | Soft natural light from above; reduce reliance on electric light by day | Consider operable or fixed; diffuse if glare on the TV is a risk |
| Ambient modern | **Dimmable recessed LED** | Even fill light; practical for cleaning and multi-use evenings | Full dimming; warm color temperature (approx. 2700–3000K) |
| Ambient retro | **LED classic “boob lights”** (mushroom / dome flush-mounts) | Nostalgic ceiling glow; grandma-home charm with efficient LED sources | On dimmers or separate scene; warm CCT to match recessed |

### Lighting principles

- Never rely on recessed cans alone — the boob lights and skylights carry the emotional and daytime layers
- Warm white LEDs only; avoid cool daylight bulbs in this room
- Support dim scenes: bright for company, soft for movies, very low for late-night TV with the faux fire on
- If skylights risk washing out the TV wall, place them over the seating / entry side rather than directly above the screen, or use shades / diffusion

## Materials and mood (palette direction)

Aligned with Design Philosophy: warm, collected, tender, familiar, restful.

- **Walls:** cream, soft butter, warm white, or gentle sage — not cold gray or stark gallery white
- **Floor:** wood or wood-look with soft underfoot path; area rug under loveseat + recliner
- **Woodwork:** painted or stained trim with enough profile to feel traditional; arch casing is a hero moment
- **Textiles:** layered throws, cushions, possibly floral or classic pattern mixed with solids
- **Decor:** books, plants, framed photos, small collections on mantel and shelves — room should accept accumulation over time

## Adjacencies

- **Front door / porch** → grand arch → living room (immediate warmth on arrival)
- Living room open or half-open toward **kitchen / dining** (see Room Program: half wall, island range) without becoming one undifferentiated box
- Clear path so guests can sit without crossing the TV sightline awkwardly

## Open questions

- Faux fireplace fuel type: electric insert, decorative only, or hybrid with real heat output?
- Mantel and surround material (wood, painted millwork, thin brick, tile)?
- Arch geometry vs. structural constraints at the entry
- Skylight count, size, and placement relative to TV glare
- Exact loveseat depth and recliner model / finish
- Whether a small media console is needed under or beside the fireplace stack
- Coordination with pellet stove location if both exist on the main floor

## Success criteria

The living room succeeds if:

1. Two people can sit comfortably (loveseat + recliner) and both see the TV without strain  
2. The faux fire + mantel still feel like a hearth when the TV is off  
3. Entering through the arch feels grand and inviting, not like a builder foyer  
4. Daylight, recessed LEDs, and boob lights each have a clear job and can be mixed  
5. The room feels warm, welcoming, and nostalgic — never sterile or trend-showroom
