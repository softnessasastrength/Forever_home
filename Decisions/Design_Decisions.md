# Design Decisions

This file records major owner decisions and the reasoning behind them. Decisions may be revised through later pull requests as the design matures.

## DD-001 — Flexible size target

**Decision:** Treat approximately 1,500 sq ft above grade as the center target rather than a hard maximum.

**Reasoning:** The house should be the smallest size that fully supports the intended life, rooms, circulation, systems, and long-term adaptability. Square footage may move up or down when justified.

## DD-002 — Raised-ranch-inspired organization

**Decision:** Use a raised-ranch or split-level-inspired form with a finished basement, main living floor, and upper private floor.

**Reasoning:** This creates clear separation between flexible/in-law space, shared daily life, and private rooms while limiting the house to three functional levels.

## DD-003 — Finished lower level

**Decision:** Finish the basement from initial construction and plan it for future in-law or long-term guest use.

**Reasoning:** A full bathroom, separate laundry room, flexible living space, garage access, and possible exterior entry allow the lower level to adapt without a major future reconstruction.

## DD-004 — Primary and supplemental heating

**Decision:** Provide central air conditioning and central forced-air heating, using natural gas where practical or oil where necessary. Add a pellet stove in the main living area.

**Reasoning:** The central system provides whole-house comfort; the pellet stove adds resilience, supplemental heat, and the warm focal point desired for the house.

## DD-005 — Electrical service baseline

**Decision:** Plan around a 200 A electrical service, subject to professional load calculations and utility approval.

**Reasoning:** The house is expected to support central HVAC, a finished lower level, EV charging, solar and battery systems, modern appliances, garage loads, and future expansion.

## DD-006 — EV charging

**Decision:** Provide one hardwired Level 2 EVSE on the left-side garage wall, with a conceptual 48 A charging target and infrastructure for a future second charger.

**Reasoning:** The left-side location matches the intended parking arrangement and keeps charging integrated with the garage service zone. Final capacity remains subject to equipment selection and load calculation.

## DD-007 — Solar and whole-home battery readiness

**Decision:** Design for a Tesla Solar Roof, Tesla photovoltaic system, or comparable future system, with a Powerwall or comparable whole-home battery near the garage-side service area.

**Reasoning:** The design should support on-site generation, backup power, and future energy management without locking the house permanently to one manufacturer.

## DD-008 — Consolidated utility zone

**Decision:** Prefer the electric meter, disconnects, solar/battery equipment, main electrical pathway, and communications demarcation on or near the side of the house closest to the garage.

**Reasoning:** Consolidating service equipment reduces unnecessary cable runs and makes maintenance clearer. Final meter and equipment locations require utility and code approval.

## DD-009 — Structured multimedia outlets

**Decision:** Provide coordinated multimedia outlet clusters at primary media locations, including power, two Ethernet ports, RG-6 coax, and upgrade pathways.

**Reasoning:** The house should support streaming, satellite, wired networking, and future technologies without routine wall demolition.

## DD-010 — Central low-voltage infrastructure

**Decision:** Home-run structured wiring to a dedicated lower-level technology area and provide empty conduit to major destinations.

**Reasoning:** Pathways, labels, access, and equipment space are the durable investment; individual network, television, satellite, and automation products will change over time.

## DD-011 — Technology neutrality

**Decision:** Brand names describe present preferences, not permanent dependencies.

**Reasoning:** Tesla, DIRECTV SWiM, and current network standards may change before construction. The house should preserve compatible space, power, wiring, grounding, and conduit for equivalent future systems.
