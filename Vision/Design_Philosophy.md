# Design Philosophy

## Purpose

The Forever Home is intended to be an extension of Branden and Renn's relationship: secure, intentional, affectionate, individual, and shared.

The design should make togetherness easy without requiring either person to give up privacy, creativity, or personal identity.

## Emotional brief

The house should feel like a beloved grandmother's home interpreted through a modern lens:

- layered rather than minimalist
- welcoming rather than impressive
- whimsical rather than chaotic
- nostalgic without becoming impractical
- full of objects, books, textiles, art, plants, and memories that accumulate meaning over time

The goal is not to manufacture age. The goal is to create a house capable of becoming more beautiful as life happens inside it.

## Relationship principles translated into architecture

### Safety

Rooms should feel legible, calm, and easy to settle into. Lighting should be layered and warm. Circulation should be simple. Storage should reduce visual chaos without erasing personality.

### Togetherness without fusion

The plan should include inviting shared spaces while giving both people a dedicated room of their own:

- Branden's office supports writing, research, design, and focused work.
- Renn's boutique supports clothing, styling, display, creativity, and self-expression.

Neither room is leftover space. Both should be treated as essential parts of the home.

### Daily rituals over grand gestures

The most important spaces are those used every day: the kitchen, living room, porch, bedroom, office, boutique, laundry, and entry sequence.

The design should create small moments of connection, such as:

- talking across a kitchen island
- reading in the same room while doing different things
- sitting together on a covered porch
- passing through a shared dressing or wardrobe zone
- seeing into a warm living space immediately upon arriving home

### Hospitality

A guest bedroom should feel genuinely welcoming, but the house should remain centered on the two people who live there.

### Permanence and adaptability

The home should be comfortable now and remain usable over decades. Favor:

- manageable scale
- minimal unnecessary level changes
- generous door clearances where feasible
- a practical bathroom layout
- durable materials that age gracefully
- rooms capable of changing function over time

## Aesthetic vocabulary

Use these words as a design check:

- warm
- collected
- tender
- bookish
- floral
- handmade
- softly colorful
- gently eccentric
- familiar
- restful
- storied

Avoid:

- cold gray interiors
- all-white sterility
- oversized empty rooms
- trend-driven finishes with no personal meaning
- harsh overhead lighting as the only light source
- layouts that sacrifice daily comfort for resale conventions

## North star

> Every room should invite people to slow down, feel safe, and become more themselves.
