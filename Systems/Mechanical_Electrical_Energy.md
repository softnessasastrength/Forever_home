# Mechanical, Electrical, and Energy Strategy

## Status

Owner design intent only. Final equipment, capacities, locations, clearances, and service sizes require professional load calculations, site information, manufacturer requirements, utility approval, and code review.

## Heating and Cooling

### Primary system

- Central air conditioning throughout the occupied house
- Central forced-air heating
- Natural gas preferred where available and practical
- Oil heat acceptable where natural gas is unavailable or unsuitable
- Zoning preferred so the lower level, main living floor, and upper private floor can be controlled separately or in sensible groups

### Supplemental heat

A pellet stove shall be located in the main living area.

The pellet stove serves three purposes:

1. Supplemental space heating
2. Resilience and reduced dependence on the primary fuel system
3. A visual and social focal point consistent with the whimsical-grandma-home aesthetic

Placement should support safe clearances, direct venting, practical cleaning, pellet delivery, and natural heat movement through the living, dining, and kitchen area. Final placement must be coordinated with structure, furniture, exterior openings, and mechanical design.

### Mechanical room

The lower-level mechanical room should include:

- Adequate service and replacement clearances
- Floor drain where permitted and appropriate
- Space for primary heating equipment and domestic water equipment
- Coordinated duct, plumbing, and electrical pathways
- Separation from living and technology equipment where required
- Access that does not require dismantling finished walls for major equipment replacement

## Electrical Service

### Service baseline

- Plan around a 200-amp residential service as the current design baseline
- Final service size requires a formal dwelling load calculation and utility coordination
- Reserve generous panel capacity for future technologies
- Prefer a main panel with ample spaces rather than minimum capacity
- Provide whole-house surge protection

### Equipment organization

Preferred arrangement:

- Electric meter and exterior service equipment on the side of the house near the garage, subject to utility approval
- Main disconnect located as required by current code and utility rules
- Main distribution panel in the lower-level mechanical/electrical area near the garage-side service entrance
- Garage subpanel for EV charging and future garage loads
- Dedicated, accessible utility/service zone rather than scattered equipment

The owner may express a preferred meter location, but the utility provider and authority having jurisdiction retain approval authority. The design should preserve an acceptable alternate location until the site and serving utility are known.

## Electric Vehicle Charging

Provide infrastructure for one regularly charged electric vehicle.

Current planning target:

- Hardwired Level 2 EVSE
- EVSE mounted on the left-side garage wall
- Conceptual target of up to 48 A charging on a dedicated 60 A branch circuit, subject to final equipment and load calculation
- Wall blocking before drywall
- Conduit and conductor pathway from garage subpanel or main panel
- Cable-management space so the charging cord does not cross a primary walkway
- Placement that can reach common front-left and rear-left vehicle charge-port locations
- Empty conduit or capacity for a future second charging position

## Solar and Battery Storage

The house shall be solar-ready and battery-ready without depending on one manufacturer.

Current owner preference:

- Tesla Solar Roof, conventional Tesla solar, or a comparable future photovoltaic system
- Tesla Powerwall or a comparable whole-home battery system
- Battery equipment preferably located on the exterior garage-side service wall or another code-compliant nearby location

Design requirements:

- Favor a large, useful solar-facing roof plane when site orientation allows
- Minimize vents, dormers, and roof penetrations on the preferred photovoltaic roof surface
- Coordinate roof structure with anticipated solar loading and attachment requirements
- Provide an accessible conduit pathway between roof/attic and the electrical service area
- Reserve wall area and working clearance for battery, inverter, disconnects, gateway, and associated equipment
- Preserve options for future bidirectional energy management
- Coordinate battery location with fire separation, egress, impact protection, ventilation, property lines, manufacturer requirements, and local code

## Resilience and Future Expansion

Plan for, without necessarily installing at initial construction:

- Generator or alternate-source transfer equipment
- Solar expansion
- Additional battery capacity
- Future heat-pump conversion or supplemental heat pumps
- Additional EV charging
- Smart load management
- Energy monitoring
- An exterior service zone that remains accessible to utility and emergency personnel

Brand names document present preferences, not permanent proprietary requirements. The infrastructure should remain adaptable if products or standards change before construction.
